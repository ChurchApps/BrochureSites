## Production Notes

### B-Roll Shots Needed:
1. ✅ Tangled cables/frustrated setup (before)
2. ✅ Clean TV mounted on wall (after)
3. ✅ Hand holding Apple TV remote
4. ✅ Kids watching TV (backs/silhouettes, bokeh)
5. ✅ Close-up of FreePlay interface on TV
6. ✅ Volunteer smiling, relaxed
7. Screen recordings of:
   - Browsing content
   - One-click download
   - Pressing play
8. Provider logos animation
9. Multiple devices (Apple TV, Fire Stick, ONN)

### Screen Recordings Needed:
- Navigate FreePlay menu
- Browse content providers
- Select and download a lesson
- Play a lesson
- Show offline indicator

### Voiceover Notes:
- Friendly, conversational tone (not overly corporate)
- Male or female voice (suggest warm, authentic voice)
- Clear enunciation for web playback
- Pace: Conversational but energetic

### Music Suggestions:
- Uplifting but not cheesy
- Modern corporate/tech feel
- Builds energy toward end
- Quiet enough to not overpower voiceover

## Technical Specs

**Video Format:** MP4 (H.264)
**Resolution:** 1920x1080 (1080p)
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Frame Rate:** 30fps
**File Size:** Under 10MB for web (use compression)
**Captions:** Include SRT file for accessibility

## Where to Place Video

**Option 1: Hero Section** (Recommended)
Replace the hero image with autoplay video (muted, loops)
- Shows immediately on page load
- Creates dynamic first impression
- Video controls optional

**Option 2: How It Works Section**
Embed as demonstration video
- Plays on user click
- Explains the process
- Can be longer (60-second version)

**Option 3: Both**
- Short loop in hero (15-30 seconds, no audio)
- Full explainer in How It Works section

If full video production isn't feasible immediately:

**30-Second Screen Recording:**
1. Record FreePlay interface on actual TV

3. Add simple text overlays:
   - "Browse lessons"
   - "One-click download"
   - "Press play"
4. Add royalty-free upbeat music
5. End with logo + "Download free on Fire TV"

**Tools:**

- Edit in iMovie/CapCut (free)
- Add text overlays and music
- Export and compress

This could be done in 1-2 hours!
