What is a ChurchApps Ambassador?

You are the organizer, not the hero. You are the connector, not the expert. You are the facilitator, not the boss.

An Ambassador IS:

  • A local community organizer for church tech volunteers
  • A safe on-ramp for churches new to ChurchApps
  • A peer supporter and learner
  • A bridge between local needs and the core team
  • A steward of a shared mission

An Ambassador is NOT:

  • A support ticket system
  • Someone who sells services
  • A replacement for the ChurchApps team
  • Someone who forks or rebrands software
  • Expected to know everything

Requirements

1. Active Christian Faith

You must be a follower of Jesus Christ and actively involved in Christian ministry or church leadership.

2. ChurchApps Experience

You must be currently using at least one ChurchApps product (Lessons.church, Worship.tools, B1.church, FreeShow, etc.) in a ministry context. You'll need to provide photos as proof.

3. References

Two references from church leaders or ministry colleagues who can vouch for your character and ministry involvement.

4. Commitment to Serve Freely

This is a volunteer position. You agree to never charge churches for ChurchApps support or services. All help must be provided freely.

5. Quarterly Activity

Host at least one user group activity per quarter (meeting, training, help session, etc.) and submit a simple quarterly report.

Important: We conduct reference checks and monitor ambassador communications to ensure integrity. Any attempt to charge for services will result in immediate removal from the program.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a paid position?

No, this is a volunteer position. Ambassadors serve freely out of a desire to help churches in their region. You may never charge for ChurchApps-related services.

How much time does it require?

At minimum, one user group activity per quarter (3 months). Most ambassadors spend 2-5 hours per month on activities, depending on how active their group becomes.

What if I'm not a technical expert?

That's okay! You're an organizer, not expected to know everything. The ChurchApps community is there to help answer technical questions together.

Can there be multiple ambassadors in one city?

Generally, we approve one ambassador per 500,000 population to ensure each can build a vibrant community. In very large cities, we may approve additional ambassadors for different districts or language groups.

What happens if I need to step down?

Life happens. If you need to pause or step down, simply let us know. This is stewardship, not a lifetime appointment. We can help find another leader or let the group rest.

Will I get a ChurchApps email address?

Yes! Upon approval, you'll receive a regional email address (e.g., korea@churchapps.org, spain@churchapps.org) that forwards to your personal email. This gives you credibility when reaching out to churches.

What support will I receive?

You'll get access to a resource hub with guides, templates, and brand assets. You'll join a private Facebook group with other ambassadors worldwide. And you'll have quarterly check-ins with the ChurchApps team.
