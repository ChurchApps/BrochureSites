üéØ Key Success Factors

1. Personal Touch

The video call with Terry/Michael is critical. This is where you:

  • Build genuine relationship
  • Get a "gut feel" for their character
  • Assess their readiness and vision
  • Answer their questions personally

2. Monitoring Without Micromanaging

The email monitoring is for scam prevention, not surveillance. Only check:

  • Weekly spot-checks of monitored mailbox
  • Any flagged keywords
  • Anonymous tips from churches

3. Low Bar, High Support

Requirements are intentionally low (1 activity per quarter) but support is high:

  • Monthly check-ins first quarter
  • Responsive to questions
  • Rich resource library
  • Peer community support

4. Photo Proof is Critical

Always require photos because:

  • Verifies actual product usage
  • Provides marketing content
  • Proves they're not just "dreamers"
  • Unlocks badges and recognition

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Pitfall #1: Skipping the video call
Don't approve anyone without the personal conversation. This is your chance to assess character and prevent problems.
Pitfall #2: Not enforcing photo requirements
Photos are non-negotiable. They prove legitimacy and provide valuable content. Don't skip this step.
Pitfall #3: Approving too many too fast
Start slow. Test with Korea. Iron out issues. Then open to 3-4 more. Then scale. Don't rush.
Pitfall #4: Ignoring quarterly reports
These reports are gold. They tell you what features to build, what problems to solve, and who's active vs. inactive.
Pitfall #5: Not monitoring emails
The whole system depends on the "no charging" policy. If you don't monitor, scammers will sneak in and damage your reputation.
